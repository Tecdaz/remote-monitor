# Apps — Surface design

**URL**: https://developer.android.com/design/ui/wear/guides/surfaces/apps
**Section**: Design → Surfaces → Apps

## What this page is

The official design guide for the **App surface** on Wear OS. Distinguishes apps from tiles/complications (richer, interactive, entered from another surface) and states the design principles + content container types specific to the full-screen app experience.

## Sections

- Principles (Focused — focus on critical tasks, seconds not minutes)
- Content container types (the building blocks: list, detail, button screen, etc.)
- Full screen takeovers (modals, confirmations, immersive flows)

## When to consult

When designing any full-screen app experience. Read this before picking container types — the choices here drive the rest of the implementation. Companion to `principles.md` (general Wear principles) and `common-layouts.md` (canonical layouts).

## Source quote

> An app is one of the primary surfaces on Wear OS. Apps are different from complications or tiles, which are glanceable representations of app content. Apps display more information and support richer interactivity. The user often enters an app from another surface, such as the launcher, recents, a notification, complication, tile, or voice action.
