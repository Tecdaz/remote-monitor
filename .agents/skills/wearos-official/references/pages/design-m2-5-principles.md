# Design principles (Wear OS, M2-5)

**URL**: https://developer.android.com/design/ui/wear/guides/m2-5/foundations/design-principles
**Section**: Design → Foundations (legacy M2-5)

## What this page is

The legacy (Material 2.5 era) but still-canonical **Wear OS design principles** page. Restated as five principles with detailed rationale: critical tasks, wrist-optimized, better together, always relevant, works offline. This is the deepest single-page articulation of the design philosophy.

## Sections

- Design for critical tasks
- Optimize for the wrist
- Better together
- Always relevant
- Works offline

## When to consult

Read once deeply. Use this page as the spec when a design choice is being debated — five named principles is the rejection test. This page is hosted under `/m2-5/` because the content predates Material 3 Expressive but the principles still apply.

## Source quote

> Wear OS is a wearable platform, helping people live more present, healthy, and productive lives. Wear OS makes smartwatches into glanceable, standalone devices, so users can stay connected online and complete tasks quickly, while leaving their phones in their pockets.
