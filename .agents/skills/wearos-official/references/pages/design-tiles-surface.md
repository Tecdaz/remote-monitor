# Tiles — Surface design

**URL**: https://developer.android.com/design/ui/wear/guides/surfaces/tiles
**Section**: Design → Surfaces → Tiles

## What this page is

The official design guide for **Tiles** on Wear OS. States the three tile principles (Immediate, Predictable, Relevant) and explains how they translate to the swipeable, non-scrollable tile surface. Companion to the developer tile guide (`pages/tiles.md`).

## Sections

- Principles
  - Immediate (purpose obvious at a glance, no delay)
  - Predictable (system-consistent design language)
  - Relevant (focused, concise, purpose-driven)

## When to consult

Before designing a tile's content layout, before naming a tile, or when reviewing whether a tile is doing too much. The three principles are the rejection test.

## Source quote

> Tiles provide quick, predictable access to information and actions to solve user needs. Since users want value without delay from their watch, the purpose of each Tile should be immediately obvious by showing only the most critical bits of information. Avoid providing a superfluous amount of functionality. Tiles should feel focused, concise, and purpose-driven.
